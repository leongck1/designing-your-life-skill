## About This Skill

This Claude skill is a personal reading companion built from personal study notes taken while reading **Designing Your Life: How to Build a Well-Lived, Joyful Life** by Bill Burnett and Dave Evans.

All frameworks, tools, exercises, and concepts in this skill are entirely derived from the book. The notes were taken as a personal learning exercise, and this skill was created to make those notes more accessible and actionable — helping Claude guide conversations using the book's methodology.

**This skill is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to the authors, Stanford University, or the official Designing Your Life programme in any way.**

If you find this skill useful, please go read the real book. It's genuinely worth it.

## The Book

### *Designing Your Life: How to Build a Well-Lived, Joyful Life*
**Bill Burnett & Dave Evans**
Published by Alfred A. Knopf (Penguin Random House), September 20, 2016
272 pages | ISBN: 9781101875322

A **#1 New York Times Bestseller** — by the authors' own count, more than 1 million copies in 24 languages.

The book grew out of a wildly popular course at Stanford University's d.school (Hasso Plattner Institute of Design), where Burnett and Evans have taught students — from fresh graduates to mid-career professionals to retirees — how to apply the same design thinking behind great technology and products to the most important design challenge of all: your own life.

The central idea is deceptively simple: designers don't agonize over the "right" answer. They prototype, test, learn, and iterate. You can do the same with your career and your life. The book gives you concrete tools to do exactly that — from building a personal compass (your Workview and Lifeview) to creating three alternative 5-year Odyssey Plans to conducting Life Design Interviews that unlock the hidden job market.

Praised by David Kelley (founder of IDEO), Daniel Pink (*Drive*), NPR's Brian Lehrer, *Publishers Weekly*, and *The New York Times* ("Life has questions. They have answers.").

## The Authors

### Bill Burnett
Bill Burnett is the Executive Director of the Design Program at Stanford University, where he oversees both the undergraduate and graduate design programmes. He holds a BS and MS in Product Design from Stanford. Before Stanford, he spent seven years at Apple as a product leader for the groundbreaking PowerBook line of laptops, and also worked in the toy industry designing Star Wars action figures. He holds multiple mechanical and design patents, and has been teaching the "Designing Your Life" course at Stanford for over a decade.

### Dave Evans
Dave Evans is the Co-Director of the Stanford Life Design Lab and an adjunct lecturer in the Product Design Program at Stanford. He holds a BS and MS in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford, as well as a graduate diploma in Contemplative Spirituality from San Francisco Theological Seminary. Early in his career he worked at Apple, where he contributed to the design of the company's first mouse, and he later co-founded **Electronic Arts (EA)**, one of the world's largest interactive entertainment companies, where he served as VP of Talent. Together with Burnett, the "Designing Your Life" course has expanded far beyond Stanford into workshops, online programmes, a coach-certification programme, and a global movement.

### Their Other Books
Following the success of *Designing Your Life*, Burnett and Evans built out a whole series:

| Book | Year | Focus |
|------|------|-------|
| *Designing Your Life* | 2016 | The original — life design methodology |
| *The Designing Your Life Workbook* | 2018 | An interactive companion with all the book's exercises (Dashboard, Compass, Good Time Journal, mind maps, Odyssey Plans) — closest companion to this skill |
| *Designing Your Work Life* | 2020 | Applying life design specifically to work and career |
| *Designing Your New Work Life* | 2021 | A revised, post-pandemic take — "Don't resign, redesign!" |

If you want to actually *do* the exercises this skill describes, the **Workbook** is the most directly useful purchase alongside the original book.

### What's in SKILL.md

SKILL.md has two parts:

1. **A coaching operating manual** — guidance on *how* to use the material in a real conversation: meet the person where they are, work one step at a time, walk exercises live rather than describing them, reframe stuck beliefs gently, bias toward action, and keep wellbeing guardrails in mind. This is what makes it behave like a coach rather than a book summary.
2. **The full toolkit, chapter by chapter** — all 11 chapters plus the introduction and conclusion:

| Chapter | Topic | Key Tool(s) |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Intro | Life by Design | 5 Design Thinking Mindsets + Dysfunctional Belief/Reframe pattern |
| 1 | Start Where You Are | Life Dashboard (Health/Work/Play/Love) |
| 2 | Building a Compass | The Three Questions, Workview, Lifeview, Integration |
| 3 | Wayfinding | Good Time Journal, AEIOU Reflection, Mining the Mountaintop |
| 4 | Getting Unstuck | Mind Mapping, Anchor Problems (Melanie, Grant) |
| 5 | Design Your Lives | Odyssey Plans (3 × 5-year) |
| 6 | Prototyping | Life Design Interviews, Prototype Experiences, Brainstorming |
| 7 | How Not to Get a Job | JD Anatomy, Resume Tips, Cool Companies caution |
| 8 | Designing Your Dream Job | Networking, Focus on Offers, Hidden Job Market |
| 9 | Choosing Happiness | 4-Step Choosing Process, Jam Study, Grokking |
| 10 | Failure Immunity | Be→Do→Become, 3-Step Failure Reframe |
| 11 | Building a Team | Team Roles, Team Rules, Counsel vs. Advice |
| Conclusion | Never-Ending Design | 5 Mindsets recap |
